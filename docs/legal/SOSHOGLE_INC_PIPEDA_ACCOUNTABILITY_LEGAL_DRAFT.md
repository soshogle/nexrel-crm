## 1. Purpose and Legal Basis

This document is intended for legal review and finalization of Soshogle Inc.'s privacy governance framework under the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA), including Fair Information Principle 1 (Accountability) and the related fair information principles referenced in the attached requirements.

This draft sets out organizational controls, policies, procedures, and operational commitments for collection, use, disclosure, retention, protection, access, correction, complaint handling, and breach response for personal information processed by Nexrel.

## 3. Scope and Data Governance Commitments

Soshogle commits to the following binding operating rules across Nexrel:

1. Collect only personal information necessary for identified, legitimate purposes.
2. Communicate purposes at or before collection in clear, understandable language.
3. Obtain valid and meaningful consent for collection, use, and disclosure, except where lawfully exempt.
4. Obtain renewed consent for materially new purposes.
5. Permit consent withdrawal subject to legal/contractual restrictions and reasonable notice, and communicate implications.
6. Limit use and disclosure to identified purposes unless legal authority requires otherwise.
7. Maintain personal information accuracy, completeness, and currency as required for intended use.
8. Apply retention and secure destruction schedules tied to purpose, legal obligations, and risk.
9. Provide access and correction rights with required timelines and procedural fairness.
10. Maintain complaint handling and incident/breach response protocols with auditable records.

## 4. Privacy Management Program Requirements

Soshogle's privacy management program shall include and maintain:

- documented privacy policies and standard operating procedures;
- records of processing activities and data inventory by system/function;
- data classification by sensitivity;
- third-party processor due diligence and contractual safeguards;
- employee privacy training and periodic refreshers;
- PIAs and threat assessments for new initiatives and technologies;
- periodic internal review and remediation tracking.

## 5. Personal Information Handling Standards

### 5.1 Collection and Consent

- Purpose specification is required prior to collection.
- Consent language must be intelligible, prominent, and specific enough for meaningful choice.
- Non-essential uses must be optional where required.

### 5.2 Use, Disclosure, and Processor Transfers

- Use/disclosure must remain within identified purposes unless legally required.
- Transfers to service providers, including outside Canada, require:
  - risk assessment,
  - contractual safeguards for comparable protection,
  - purpose limitation,
  - transparency notice to affected individuals.

### 5.3 Retention and Destruction

- Retention schedules must be documented by data category.
- Disposal must be secure, auditable, and aligned to legal hold requirements.

### 5.4 Accuracy

- Procedures must support timely correction and dispute notation.
- Where appropriate, corrected data is propagated to relevant third parties.

## 6. Individual Rights Procedure (Access/Correction/Deletion Requests)

Soshogle shall provide procedures to:

- receive and log requests;
- verify requester identity;
- locate and provide requested information in understandable format;
- respond within 30 days, with one permissible extension up to 30 additional days where lawful;
- issue written reasons when access is denied and provide recourse information;
- process correction requests and note unresolved disputes;
- maintain request records for accountability and audit.

## 7. Complaints and Challenges to Compliance

Soshogle shall maintain a complaint workflow that:

- records receipt date and complaint nature;
- acknowledges receipt promptly;
- assigns impartial reviewer(s) with sufficient authority and access;
- communicates outcome and corrective measures promptly;
- tracks systemic remediation and policy updates.

## 8. Security Controls and Encryption Posture

Soshogle commits to safeguards appropriate to sensitivity, including:

- encryption in transit (HTTPS/TLS with strict transport settings);
- encryption at rest for sensitive data stores and files;
- field-level encryption for secrets and sensitive tokens where applicable;
- role-based access controls and audit logging for sensitive actions;
- incident response and breach documentation.

Implementation note for legal review: "end-to-end encryption" in a strict cryptographic sense (only endpoints can decrypt with no server access) is not universally applicable to all SaaS workflows. For Nexrel, counsel should validate wording as "encryption in transit and at rest with strict key management and least-privilege access controls" unless a specific workflow is architected for true E2EE.

## 9. Breach and Incident Management

Soshogle shall maintain a breach protocol that includes:

- incident triage and severity classification;
- containment and forensic preservation;
- documented risk-of-harm assessment;
- notification actions required by applicable law;
- remediation and post-incident control improvements.

## 10. Public Transparency and Internal Availability

Soshogle shall ensure privacy policies and procedures are:

- publicly available in plain language for customers;
- available internally for employees and contractors;
- version-controlled and periodically reviewed.
