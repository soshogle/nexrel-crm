# AI Employees & AI Team — Training Manual

A simple guide to understanding how AI employees work, how they connect to workflows, and what Auto-Run does.

---

## What Are AI Employees?

**AI Employees** are virtual assistants that work for your business 24/7. They can:

- Make and take phone calls
- Send SMS and emails
- Follow up with leads and patients
- Remind people about appointments
- Handle routine tasks automatically

Think of them like staff members who never sleep and can handle many routine tasks at once.

---

## The Three Types of AI Teams

Your business may have one or more of these:

### 1. AI Team (Professional)

General-purpose assistants for any business:

- **AI Accountant** — Invoicing, expense tracking
- **AI Developer** — Code reviews, documentation
- **AI Admin Assistant** — Scheduling, data entry
- **AI Sales Rep** — Lead follow-ups, proposals
- And more…

### 2. RE Team (Real Estate)

Specialized for real estate:

- **Speed to Lead** — Reaches out to new leads within minutes
- **Sphere Nurture** — Stays in touch with your sphere
- **Buyer Follow-up** — Follows up with buyers
- And more…

### 3. Industry Team (Dental, Medical, Restaurant, etc.)

Built for your specific industry:

- **Appointment Scheduler** — Sends reminders, confirms appointments
- **Patient Coordinator** — Welcomes new patients, follows up
- **Treatment Coordinator** — Follows up on treatment plans
- **Billing Specialist** — Sends invoice reminders

## How Do AI Employees Work?

### Step 1: Set Up (Provision)

Before an AI employee can work, you need to:

1. **Assign a voice** — Choose how they sound on phone calls
2. **Assign a phone number** (optional) — So they can make and receive calls

This is called **provisioning**. Until you do this, the AI employee is just a card — it can’t do anything yet.

### Step 2: Choose How They Run

There are **two main ways** an AI employee does work:

| Method | When it runs | Best for |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| **Auto-Run** | Automatically when something happens (e.g. new lead) | Reacting to new leads, new patients, new inquiries |
| **Scheduled / Manual** | At a set time each day, or when you click “Run now” | Daily reminders, follow-ups, batch outreach |

---

## What Is Auto-Run?

**Auto-Run** is a switch that says: *“When a new lead or contact comes in, start this AI employee’s workflow automatically.”*

### In Plain English

- **Auto-Run OFF** — The AI employee only runs when you schedule it or click “Run now”
- **Auto-Run ON** — The AI employee springs into action as soon as a new lead is created

### Example

You turn on Auto-Run for **Speed to Lead** (Real Estate):

- A buyer fills out a form on your website
- **Immediately**, the system starts the Speed to Lead workflow
- The AI sends an SMS, then calls the lead — all without you doing anything

---

## How Does Auto-Run Connect to Workflows?

### The Simple Version

1. **Workflow** = A sequence of steps (e.g. Send SMS → Wait 30 min → Make call → Send email)
2. **Auto-Run** = The trigger that starts that workflow when a new lead arrives
3. When you turn **Auto-Run ON**, the system links that AI employee to a workflow

### What Happens When You Turn Auto-Run ON

1. You toggle **Auto-Run** to ON for an AI employee (e.g. Appointment Scheduler)
2. The system checks: *“Does this employee have a workflow?”*
3. If **no** → It creates a default workflow for you (e.g. welcome SMS, follow-up call)
4. If **yes** → It links that existing workflow to the AI employee
5. From now on, **every new lead** automatically starts that workflow

### The Flow (Visual)

```
New lead comes in (form, widget, etc.)
         ↓
System checks: "Is Auto-Run ON for any AI employee?"
         ↓
Yes → Find the workflow linked to that employee
         ↓
Start the workflow (SMS, call, email, etc.)
         ↓
AI employee does its job automatically
```

## How Do AI Employees and Workflows Work Together?

### Workflows Define *What* Happens

A workflow is a recipe. It says things like:

- Step 1: Send welcome SMS
- Step 2: Wait 30 minutes
- Step 3: Make a follow-up call
- Step 4: Send an email

### AI Employees Are *Who* Does It

Each AI employee is assigned to certain steps in a workflow. For example:

- **Speed to Lead** might handle the call step
- **Appointment Scheduler** might handle the SMS step

### Auto-Run Is the *When* (Trigger)

Auto-Run says: *“Start this workflow as soon as a new lead arrives.”*

## Manage Tasks vs. Auto-Run

These are **two different ways** to control your AI employees:

### Manage Tasks (On the AI Employee Card)

- **Where:** Click an AI employee → **Manage Tasks**
- **What it does:**
  - Turn individual tasks on or off (e.g. “Send appointment reminders”)
  - Set a daily schedule (e.g. “Run at 5pm every day”)
  - Customize SMS and email messages
  - Add custom tasks (e.g. “Send insurance claims daily at 5pm”)
  - Click **Run now** to run manually

- **When it runs:** On a schedule you set, or when you click “Run now”

### Auto-Run (On the AI Employee Card)

- **Where:** Toggle on the AI employee card
- **What it does:** Starts a workflow automatically when a new lead is created
- **When it runs:** The moment a new lead comes in (form, widget, etc.)

### Quick Comparison

| | Manage Tasks | Auto-Run |
|---|--------------|----------|
| **Trigger** | Schedule or manual | New lead created |
| **Use case** | Daily reminders, batch outreach | Instant response to new leads |
| **You control** | Time, message content, which tasks | Whether it runs at all on new leads |

## Summary: The Big Picture

1. **AI Employees** = Virtual staff that make calls, send messages, and follow up
2. **AI Team / RE Team / Industry Team** = Different types of AI employees for different needs
3. **Workflows** = The sequence of steps (SMS, call, email) that run automatically
4. **Auto-Run** = The switch that starts a workflow when a new lead arrives
5. **Manage Tasks** = Where you control schedules, messages, and manual runs

**In one sentence:**  
*AI employees do the work; workflows define the steps; Auto-Run decides when to start.*
